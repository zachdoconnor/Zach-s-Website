About Me

I'm a recent graduate from Indiana University's Luddy School of Informatics. My passion for technology was developed while taking a coding elective, leading me to fall in love with building websites using HTML and CSS. This experience shaped my decision to pursue a bachelor's degree in Informatics, combining my interests in technology and its interaction with people.

During my undergraduate studies, I had the opportunity to intern at VMS BioMarketing. Here, I furthered the development of my technical skills in engineering and data analysis, playing a key role in developing and testing a new Salesforce Org to enhance efficiency and functionality. My work also involved setting up a foundation for a marketing system in Salesforce, strengthening VMS’s organization and outreach strategies. This experience not only improved my technical skills but also my communication and teamwork abilities.

After graduating, my interest in how business and technology influence each other led me to continue my education at the Kelley School of Business. I'm currently pursuing a master's degree in Information Systems (MSIS), a challenge I embraced to bridge the gap between technology and business. The program, known for its intensity, has already shown me significant growth in my professional capabilities.

In my personal life, I'm a proud Indianapolis native (Go Colts!) and an active member of my local community. When not immersed in school work, I enjoy playing golf, hitting the gym, and spending quality time at the dog park with my pug.

As I venture into the professional world, I aim to leverage my expertise in data analysis and business acumen to create impactful solutions in the workplace. I am eager to contribute to diverse and innovative teams, fostering an environment of mutual growth and success. I believe that my unique blend of technical knowledge and business insight will be an asset in tackling complex business challenges through technology.
